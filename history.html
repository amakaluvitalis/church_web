History of the Anglican Church of Kenya

Explore the journey of our church through the years, from humble beginnings to a thriving community of faith and service.

1844 - Beginnings of the Anglican Church of Kenya

The Anglican Church of Kenya traces its origins to the arrival of Dr. Johann Ludwig Krapf in 1844, who, through the Church Missionary Society, established the first mission station in Rabai-Coast. His efforts, along with those of other early missionaries, laid the foundation for the spread of the gospel in Kenya.

1961 - Expansion and Formation of New Dioceses

In 1961, the Anglican Church of Kenya achieved a significant milestone with the establishment of new dioceses. The Dioceses of Fort Hall (now Murang’a), Maseno, and Nakuru were carved out of the initial solitary Diocese of Mombasa, marking the beginning of structured regional church administration.

This period also saw a rapid transformation in the church’s organizational structure, leading to the eventual establishment of the Diocese of Nairobi in 1964. These changes allowed for better pastoral care, evangelism, and governance, ensuring the steady growth of the Anglican community in Kenya.

1970 - Establishment of the Diocese of Maseno South

The year 1970 marked a historic moment with the official establishment of the Diocese of Maseno South. This milestone was crucial in the decentralization of the Anglican Church’s leadership in the western region of Kenya, enabling greater focus on evangelism, pastoral care, and community development in Maseno and surrounding areas.

Rev. Evan Agola became the first bishop of Maseno South (1970-1973), laying the foundation for strong church governance and expansion. His successor, Rev. Dr. Henry Okullu (1974-1994), further shaped the diocese’s mission, advocating for both spiritual growth and social justice, making the church a key pillar in the region’s development.

Since its establishment, Maseno South Diocese has expanded significantly, leading to the creation of additional dioceses such as Maseno East, Maseno West, and the Diocese of Bondo, further strengthening the Anglican Church’s presence in western Kenya.

1980s - Founding of All Saints’ Church, Maseno

The original Anglican Church in Maseno, known as Rock of Ages, was built near the present-day Maseno School. For many years, it served as the home of both the Diocese of Maseno South and Maseno North, playing a crucial role in shaping the church's presence in the Kavirondo region.

By the 1980s, the growing needs of the Anglican community led to the founding and construction of All Saints’ Church, Maseno, which now serves as the parish headquarters. This marked a new chapter in the church’s development, enabling it to focus on expanding its evangelical and pastoral services in the region.

Today, All Saints’ Church leads Maseno Parish, which includes six other churches: ACK St. Andrews Mbalawandu, ACK St. James Marera, ACK St. Johns Ong’iyo, ACK St. Anne’s Huma, ACK Mama Rebecca Memorial Church-Nyawita, and ACK Christ the King-Mirunda. However, compared to other churches in the diocese, All Saints’ Church has faced challenges in evangelistic outreach and infrastructural development, requiring renewed efforts for growth and sustainability.

Present Day - Growth and Leadership of ACK Maseno

Since its establishment, the Diocese of Maseno South has played a crucial role in the expansion of the Anglican Church in Kenya. The diocese has been led by notable bishops, including Rev. Evan Agola (1970-1973), Rev. Dr. Henry Okullu (1974-1994), Rev. Mwai Abiero (1994-2018), and Rev. Charles Ong’injo (2018-present).

The diocese currently consists of five archdeaconries—Cathedral, Maseno, Ndiru, Ngere, and St. Luke’s—governing a total of 39 parishes. St. Stephens Cathedral in Kisumu City serves as its central place of worship and administration.

Over the years, five new dioceses have been created from Maseno South: Maseno East, Maseno West, Bondo, Southern Nyanza, and Kisii. This growth reflects the diocese’s strong evangelistic mission and dedication to spreading the gospel. Today, the Anglican Church of Kenya has 37 dioceses across the country, under the leadership of Rev. Dr. Jackson Ole Sapit, the current Archbishop.

The history of the Anglican Church in Kenya cannot be told without acknowledging Maseno, where it all began. The name "Maseno" itself originates from the large deciduous indigenous tree (Cordia monoica) called “Oseno” in Luo and “Luseno” in Luhya, under which the first Anglican Church services in the region were held as early as 1906.

2024 - Digital Transformation and Future Vision

The evangelical sphere is rapidly evolving with advancements in technology and infrastructure, and ACK All Saints’ Church-Maseno is embracing this transformation. The church is now focusing on sustainable resource mobilization beyond traditional offertory, tithes, and donations to meet its expanding financial obligations.

As part of its 2024-2028 Strategic Plan, the church aims to integrate digital platforms into worship and evangelism, making services more accessible to congregants both physically and online. This includes broadcasting services live, utilizing social media for outreach, and adopting modern technology to enhance liturgy and administration.

In addition to digital expansion, the church remains committed to fostering discipleship, compassion, and stewardship within the Anglican community. Congregants are encouraged to actively participate in ministries such as Sunday school, youth ministry, choir, KAMA, Mothers’ Union, and lay readership, ensuring holistic spiritual growth.

This strategic plan serves as a roadmap for the church's future, guiding socio-economic transformation while remaining rooted in Anglican traditions and biblical principles. As emphasized in Ephesians 6:10, ACK All Saints’ Church-Maseno is committed to equipping its members with the full armor of God to navigate the changing world with faith and purpose.

- Rev. Emma Omondi,
